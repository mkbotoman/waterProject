About the project

Recent climate trends pose a threat to US water resources and highlight the need for more intelligent water management. While utilities have begun to add intelligence to potable water supply and consumption, data-driven management is needed in all parts of the water cycle. Increased flooding, mudslides, algae blooms and pollution discharges into urban waterways have had both environmental and economic costs. Municipalities are addressing these stormwater concerns through large infrastructure improvements at high costs that have diminishing returns. This project will explore the potential for distributed monitoring and control devices built on commodity hardware to aid in stormwater management at a low-cost. These networked devices will interface with existing utility controls via Modbus to enable high-resolution data acquisition and control.
